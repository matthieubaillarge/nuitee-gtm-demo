# GTM Engineer

**Interview Prep Guide for** **GTM Engineer** **(Growth)**

A few pointers to help you prepare for this first conversation, including the case study section you'll need to bring.

## Interview process

1. **This interview, with Gian Caprini (VP Growth):** conversation plus presentation of the Customer Journey part of the case study.
2. **Technical interview:** SQL, APIs, data tooling, and how you use AI day to day.
3. **Final interview:** the remaining parts of the case study.

## Who you'll be speaking with

Gian Caprini, VP Growth, and your future manager if hired.

## 📋 Case study to prepare

> **Customer Journey**
> 

> Nuitee Connect (LiteAPI) enables travel companies to access hotel inventory, content, and booking capabilities through a single API. Review the journey from initial interest to an active Connect customer. Explain where you would focus your attention, what challenges or opportunities you would investigate, and how you would prioritize improvements.
> 

> **Customer Journey:** Review the journey from initial interest to an active Connect (LiteAPI) customer. Explain where you would focus your attention, what challenges or opportunities you would investigate, and how you would prioritize improvements.
> 

> **Format:** slides, document, or a vibe coded solution, whatever you're most comfortable with.
> 

> **Time:** 5 to 10 minutes.
> 

## What we're looking for

- A GTM and business mind, someone who connects data to real decisions, not just reporting.
- Genuine, specific knowledge of Nuitee and Connect (LiteAPI).
- Comfort building things from ambiguity, without an existing playbook to lean on.
- Technical fluency (SQL, APIs, tools like HubSpot, PostHog, Clay, ZoomInfo, Looker) and real, daily use of AI in your workflow.
- Ownership mentality, treating this like your own business rather than needing structure to operate.

## Quick tips

- Rehearse the presentation and keep it within the time window.
- Do real research on Nuitee beforehand. Generic answers about the business will stand out in a bad way.
- Good moment to ask Gian about the day to day and anything you're curious about regarding how the team is structured.
